## Ecosystems:

Ecosystem - A natural system made up of both biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components.
-> It can include things such as water, rocks and dead biomass
-> It has inputs, outputs and processes

Producers - Produce biomass from photosynthesis (eg. grass, algae)
Primary consumers - Herbivores that consume producers (eg. cows, some fish)
Secondary consumers - Carnivores/omnivores that consume primary consumers (eg. birds, humans)
Decomposers - Funghi, insects or bacteria that break down dead biomass to their constituent nutrients (eg. larvae, maggots)

Food chain - Connections between organisms that rely upon each other as their source of food
Food web - A complex hierarchy of organisms relying on each other for food; a set of interconnected food chains

Due to their interdependence, the removal of just one or two species can have catastrophic consequences in the long run.

## Biomes:

Biome - A type of environment. A large-scale ecosystem
-> A biome's location is dependant on climate, altitude and proximity to water
-> They form latitudinal belts across the world

Tropical - 15°-30° N/S of the equator. Distinguished by wet/dry seasons. High temperature (average never below 18°).
Dry - Very little precipitation. Found at roughly 30° N/S of the equator.
Temperate/continental - 40°-60° N/S of the equator. Characterised by large variations in seasons.
Polar - Extremely cold (colder than -50°). Found in the arctic and Antarctica.

## Tropical Rainforests:

Temperature: 20°C-30°C
Precipitation: 2-10m per year

The climate at the equator is so hot as it receives the highest amount of energy per land area. This creates a zone of very hot and moist air, making it rise and cool, condensing and then descending as rain. This creates a permanent low pressure zone over the equator.
