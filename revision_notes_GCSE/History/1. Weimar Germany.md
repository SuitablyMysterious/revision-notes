## Germany in 1918:

#### Political:

- The Kaiser (Wilheim), was losing control of his government.
- Wilheim eventually abdicates, only after the army stops supporting him.
- Fighting starts between the far-left and far-right over who takes power.
- Real risk of Germany dissolving into separate states. At one point Munich declares itself as a communist state.

#### Social:

- The working class resents the "bourgeois" for profiting out of the war when they lost out.
- Influx of 1.5 million demobalised soldiers, who thought they were winning the war.
- 750 thousand people died from famine.

#### Economic:

- 1/3 of the average earnings in 1913.
- Government debt tripled to 150 billion marks.
- Huge earnings disparity between rich and poor.

## Surrender to the Allies:

On the 11th of November 1918, Ebert's representative, Matthias Erzberger signed an armistice with the US, France and England. 
-> The German public were outraged, and, thinking they were winning the war, named the politicians who arranged this the "*November Criminals*".
-> They also blamed the Jews, Communists and Politicians. This theory became known as "*Dolchstoss*" or the "*stab in the back*" myth.

Erzberger was assassinated by 2 navy officers hired by the far right.

## The Treaty of Versailles:

**T** - Territory
**R** - Reparations
**A** - Armaments
**W** - War Guilt
**L** - League of Nations

___Territory___:
-> Germany lost *all* of her overseas territories
-> Much of Germany was given to Poland, France and Denmark. The Baltic states were formed.

___Reparations:___
-> 136,000 million marks owed to the allies (£6.6 million - worth £443 billion today)
-> Paid back in yearly instalments until 1988

___Armaments:___
-> Army restricted to 100,000 men
-> No conscription
-> No tanks, submarines or aircraft
-> Demilitarise the Rhineland

___War Guilt:___
-> Germany forced to sign Clause 231 which pinned the entire blame of WW1 on Germany
-> The ToV was known as a "*diktat*", meaning a dictation

___League of Nations:___
-> This was set up but excluded Germany
-> A major blow to national pride
