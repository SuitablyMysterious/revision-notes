### Magnetic Poles:

* All Permanent magnets have 2 magnetic poles
* When a magnet is split, you get 2 weaker magnets

### Permanent and Induced Magnetism:

Permanent magnets can induce a magnetic field in other magnetic metals. They do this by aligning the magnetic domains within the substance. An Electromagnet is a type of induced magnet.

### Magnetic Fields:

*Magnetic Field* - A region around a magnet where a magnetic material experiences a force

* Field lines are continuous and do not cross
* Field lines are symmetrical
* The higher the concentration of field lines, the stronger the magnetic field
* Field lines go from NORTH to SOUTH

Note: the 'north' end of a compass follows the field line from north to south (i.e. it points to the 'south' pole).

### Electromagnetism:

*Electromagnet* - a magnet made with a coil, core and a power supply
*Solenoid* - a wire would round in loops and looks like a spring

*Static* charge doesn't effect magnets, however *flowing* charge does.

*Fleming's right hand rule* - Put out your right hand with your thumb pointing in the direction of current, your fingers point the magnetic field direction

The strength of an electromagnet depends on a couple of factors:
1. Number of coils
2. Coil diameter
3. Core material
4. Coil current
5. Coil frequency

### Using Electromagnets:

*Speakers* - Electrical data in the form of a sound wave is converted into changes in voltage => This is then fed into a coil around a permanent magnet that moves a diaphragm => the changes in voltage correspond to a change in magnetic field strength, making the diaphragm vibrate and produce sound waves

### The Motor Effect:

*Fleming's left hand rule* - 1st finger gives field; 2nd gives current; thumb gives movement
