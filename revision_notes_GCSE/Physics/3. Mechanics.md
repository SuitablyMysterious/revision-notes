
### Distance, Speed, Acceleration:

*Distance* - the total length of a path an object travels (scalar-magnitude) \[$m$]
*Displacement* - the change in position from start to finish (vector-magnitude) \[$m$]
*Speed* - how fast an object is travelling \[$m/s$]

$$Speed = Distance * Time^{-1}$$
$$v = st^{-1}$$
or:
$$v = \Delta s\Delta t^{-1}$$

*Acceleration* - the change in speed over time \[$ms^{-2}$]
$$\Delta v = \dfrac{\Delta s_1 \Delta t_1^{-1}}{\Delta s_2 \Delta t_2^{-1}}$$
or:
$$a = (u - v)t^{-1}$$

$$Gradient = \dfrac{change\ in\ y}{change\ in\ x}$$
$$Gradient = \Delta y \Delta x^{-1}$$
$$Gradient = \Delta y \Delta x^{-1} = \Delta distance \Delta time^{-1} = ms^{-1}$$
$$Gradient = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{\Delta distance}{\Delta time} = m/s$$

### Forces:

A resultant force will cause an object to either accelerate, deform or change direction.
A lack of a resultant force causes an object to not change velocity, shape or direction.

#### Newton's Laws:

1.  An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion remains in motion at constant speed and in a straight line unless acted on by an unbalanced force.
2. The acceleration of an object depends on the mass of the object and the amount of force applied.
3. Whenever one object exerts a force on another object, the second object exerts an equal and opposite on the first.

### Weight:

$$Weight[N] = mass[kg] * gravity[NKg^{-1}]$$
$$W = mg$$
