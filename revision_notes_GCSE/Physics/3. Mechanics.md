
### Distance, Speed, Acceleration:

*Distance* - the total length of a path an object travels (scalar-magnitude) \[$m$]
*Displacement* - the change in position from start to finish (vector-magnitude) \[$m$]
*Speed* - how fast an object is travelling \[$m/s$]

$$Speed = Distance * Time^{-1}$$
$$v = st^{-1}$$
or:
$$v = \Delta s\Delta t^{-1}$$

*Acceleration* - the change in speed over time \[$ms^{-2}$]
$$\Delta v = \dfrac{\Delta s_1 \Delta t_1^{-1}}{\Delta s_2 \Delta t_2^{-1}}$$
or:
$$a = (u - v)t^{-1}$$

$$Gradient = \dfrac{change\ in\ y}{change\ in\ x}$$
$$Gradient = \Delta y \Delta x^{-1}$$
$$Gradient = \Delta y \Delta x^{-1} = \Delta distance \Delta time^{-1} = ms^{-1}$$
$$Gradient = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{\Delta distance}{\Delta time} = m/s$$
