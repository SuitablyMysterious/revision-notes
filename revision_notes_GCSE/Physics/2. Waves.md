### Transverse Waves:

![[Pasted image 20260414184436.png]]
Oscillations in transverse waves are perpendicular to the direction of travel.

Light is a transverse wave (although can be classed as a particle with particle-wave duality) that travels at $3*10^8 ms^{-1}$.
S waves are transverse.

#### Equations:
$$Frequency = Period^{-1}$$
$$Period = Frequency^{-1}$$

$$Frequency = Number_{Waves}*Time^{-1}$$
$$F = N_wT^{-1}$$


$$Speed = Distance*Time^{-1}$$
$$V=st^{-1}$$



### Longitudinal Waves:
![[Pasted image 20260414190525.png|473]]
![[Pasted image 20260414190418.png|465]]

Oscillations in longitudinal waves are parallel to the direction of travel.

Sound is a longitudinal wave that travels at $3.4*10^2 ms^{-1}$. Humans can hear between frequencies of 20Hz to 20,000Hz.
P waves are longitudinal.

![[Pasted image 20260414193722.png]]
$$\Theta_i = \Theta_r$$
Specular reflection is reflection from a smooth surface in a single direction.
Diffuse reflection is reflection from a rough surface in multiple direction.

![[Pasted image 20260414194120.png]]
