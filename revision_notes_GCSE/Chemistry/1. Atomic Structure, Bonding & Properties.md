### Names and Symbols of Elements:

*Atom* - smallest part of an element that can exist
*Element* - contains only 1 type of atom
↳ a symbol of an element represents one atom of said element; or natural molecular form
*Molecule* - a number of atoms bonded together
↳ halogens are so reactive that they bond with themselves forming *diatomic* molecules ($O_2$, $I_2$, etc.)
*Compound* - 2 or more elements chemically bonded in a fixed ratio (meaning $H_2O$ will never be $H_4O_2$)

### Atomic Model:
| Particle | Place in atom | Mass (relative) | Charge (relative) |
|:--------:|:-------------:|:---------------:|:-----------------:|
| proton   | inside        | 1               | +1                |
| neutron  | inside        | 1               | 0                 |
| electron | in shells     | 1/2000          | -1                |
The radius of an atom is $0.1 \mu m$ - $1 * 10^{-10}m$
The radius of an atom's nucleus is $1/10000$th of an atom - $1 * 10^{-14}m$

$No_{protons} = No_{electrons}$ because an atom is neutral in charge by definition.

*Electron* - subatomic particle with a charge -1. They orbit the nucleus in electron shells due to electrostatic forces of attraction.

### Isotopes:

*Isotope* - an atom of an element with a different number of neutrons.

$$Relative\ atomic\ mass = \dfrac{sum\ of\ (isotope\ abundance * isotope\ mass\ number)}{sum\ of\ isotope\ abundance}$$
$$A_r = \dfrac{\Sigma(A_iM_i)}{\Sigma(A_1)}$$
### Electronic Structure:

An electron shell can only hold a certain number of electrons. They are filled up from the nucleus outwards.
↳ Shell 1 - $2\bar{e}$
↳ Shell 2 - $8\bar{e}$
↳ Shell 3 - $8\bar{e}$
↳ Shell 4 - $10\bar{e}$

### Forming Ions:

Atoms with incomplete outer shells are unstable; when they react they "try" to gain or lose electrons to form a full outer shell - this makes them an ion.

*Ion* - An atom with a charge after gaining or losing electrons to form a full outer shell.

$$Mg[2,8,2] \to  Mg^{2+}[2,8]^{2+}$$

### Chemical Reactions:

$$ Chlorine\ Atom + Electron \to Chloride\ Ion$$
$$Cl + \bar{e} \to Cl^-$$
$$[2,8,7]+\bar{e} \to [2,8,8]^-$$
### Ionic Bonding:

When elements bond ionically, they exchange electrons in order to gain a full outer shell. Then, electrostatic forces of attraction hold the ions together in a lattice.

$$2Na + Cl_2 \to 2Na^-\ (2*(Na^-+\bar{e))}+ 2Cl^+\ (2*(Cl^+-\bar{e})) \to 2NaCl$$

### Ionic Compounds:

All compounds of similar bonding have similar chemical and physical properties. For ionic compounds (specifically giant ionic lattices), these are:
- Soluble in water
  ↳ $NaCl + H_2O(H_2^{\delta +}O^{\delta -}) \to Na^+ + CL^- (NaCl_{(aq)}) + H_2O$
- High melting an boiling point
  ↳ Due to strong electrostatic forces of attraction
- Electrical conductivity - only when liquid or gaseous
  ↳ Requires free electrons or ions

### Covalent Bonding:

Covalent bonds form between 2 or more non-metal atoms. Because non-metal atoms form negative ions, they don't lose electrons, instead they "share" electrons.
$$ H_2O$$
$$\mathrm{H-O-H}$$
### Giant Covalent Structures:

- Alletropes of carbon:
  ↳ Graphite - conducts electricity
  ↳ Diamond - very hard

Properties:
- High melting point
- Non conductive (except for graphite/ene)
- Hard
- Brittle

*Fullerenes* - Hollow 3D structures made from covalently bonded carbon atoms.

### Metallic Bonding:

Metals bond together to form Giant Metallic Structures. These consist of metal ions (positive) and a sea of delocalised electrons. For this reason, metals are often ductile and malleable.

Roman numerals are used to denote the group the metal behaves as. for example:
$$Ag(I) \to Ag^++\bar{e}$$
### Alloys:

*Alloy* - a mixture of metals blended together. Differently sized atoms disrupt the layers and make it harder.

Bronze = Copper + Tin
Brass = Copper + Zinc
Steel = Iron + Zinc + Carbon

### Corrosion and Rusting:

*Corrosion* - the chemical process that degrades metals due to contact with air or water.
*Rusting* -
$$iron + oxygen + water \to iron(III)\ oxide.\ pentahydrate$$
$$4Fe + 3O_2 + 10H_2O \to 2Fe_2O_3.10H_2O$$

### Nanomaterials:

*Nanoparticles* - Particles with diameters from 1 to 100 nanometers. This makes them 10 to 1000 times bigger than an atom.

They are useful due to their large surface area to volume ratio.
↳ Because of this they can be used in smaller amounts that when bulk materials would be used.

### Development of the Periodic Table:

**Early attempts to classify elements**

- Scientists tried grouping elements based on similar properties
- Early systems were inconsistent and incomplete

**Döbereiner’s triads (1829)**

- Johann Wolfgang Döbereiner grouped elements in threes (triads)
- The middle element had properties roughly the average of the other two
- Worked for some elements but not all

**Newlands’ octaves (1864)**

- John Newlands arranged elements by increasing atomic mass
- Noticed properties repeated every 8 elements (law of octaves)
- Pattern only worked for lighter elements and was not widely accepted

**Mendeleev’s table (1869)**

- Dmitri Mendeleev arranged elements by atomic mass and properties
- Left gaps for undiscovered elements
- Correctly predicted properties of missing elements
- His table became widely accepted

**Discovery of new elements**

- New elements were found that fit Mendeleev’s predictions
- Helped confirm the validity of his table

**Modern periodic table**

- Henry Moseley showed elements should be ordered by atomic number, not mass
- This resolved inconsistencies in Mendeleev’s table
- The modern table is arranged by increasing atomic number and recurring properties
